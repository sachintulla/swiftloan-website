The mark & wordmark

The exact ₹ symbol leans forward ~7° with three trailing speed streaks — money in motion — inside the 145° teal→green tile. Wordmark is Public Sans 800, "Swift" in teal, "Loan" in ink (mint + white on dark).

03 · Typography

Type system

Public Sans for display & headings (400–800, tight tracking). Inter for body & UI (400–700). JetBrains Mono for code & values. Numbers use tabular figures; currency is ₹ with Indian grouping (₹1,00,000).

05 · Glass & background

Liquid glass surfaces

07 · Usage

Do & don't

check
Keep the streaks behind the ₹. They read left-to-right as motion — the rupee arriving fast.
check
Light & airy by default. Soft off-white backgrounds, generous whitespace, large diffuse shadows.
check
Trust everywhere. Encryption notes, RBI/compliance badges, "no impact on credit score".
close
Don't recolour the ₹, rotate the tile, use pure black text, or make surfaces opaque grey boxes.
